# Sheet Pan Lemon Chicken with Sweet Potatoes and Avocado Goddess Sauce

## Ingredients
- [ ] 1.5 lbs chicken thighs or breasts
- [ ] 2 large sweet potatoes, cubed
- [ ] 4 garlic cloves, minced
- [ ] 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
- [ ] 2 teaspoons cumin
- [ ] 2 teaspoons chili powder
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes
- [ ] 1 teaspoon sea salt
- [ ] 1/4 cup olive oil
- [ ] 2 lemons, juiced and zested
- [ ] Fresh cilantro and parsley for garnish

### Avocado Goddess Sauce
- [ ] 1 avocado, ripe
- [ ] 1/4 cup Greek yogurt or tahini
- [ ] 2 tablespoons fresh parsley
- [ ] 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro
- [ ] 1 clove garlic
- [ ] 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- [ ] 2 tablespoons olive oil
- [ ] Salt and pepper to taste

## Directions

### 1. Prepare Marinade and Vegetables
1. Preheat oven to 425°F
2. In a large bowl, mix together minced garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, chili powder, chili flakes, sea salt, olive oil, and lemon juice
3. Cube sweet potatoes into 1-inch pieces
4. Toss chicken and sweet potatoes with the marinade until well coated

### 2. Roast
1. Arrange chicken and sweet potatoes in a single layer on a large sheet pan
2. Roast for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until chicken is cooked through (165°F internal temp)
3. Sweet potatoes should be tender and slightly caramelized

### 3. Make Avocado Goddess Sauce
1. In a food processor, blend avocado, Greek yogurt (or tahini), parsley, cilantro, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, salt and pepper
2. Blend until smooth and creamy
3. Add water if needed to reach desired consistency

### 4. Serve
1. Serve chicken and sweet potatoes hot
2. Drizzle with avocado goddess sauce
3. Garnish with fresh cilantro and parsley
4. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over everything

## Tips
- Cut sweet potatoes in uniform sizes for even cooking
- Don't overcrowd the sheet pan - use two pans if needed
- For spicier dish, increase the chili flakes
- Can substitute Greek yogurt with tahini for dairy-free version
- Check chicken internal temperature reaches 165°F for food safety

## Notes
- Serves 4-6 people
- Prep time: 15 minutes, Cook time: 30 minutes
- Perfect weeknight dinner - one pan meal
- Avocado goddess sauce can be made ahead and stored in fridge
