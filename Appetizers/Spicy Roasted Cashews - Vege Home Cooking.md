# Spicy Roasted Cashews (Masala Kaju)

## Ingredients
- [ ] 2 cups raw cashews
- [ ] 1 tablespoon ghee or coconut oil
- [ ] 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon red chili powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon chaat masala (or substitute garam masala)
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon amchur powder (dry mango powder) - optional
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- [ ] Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish (optional)

## Directions

### 1. Prepare the Cashews
1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C)
2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper
3. Melt ghee or coconut oil in a large bowl

### 2. Coat and Season
1. Add all spices (turmeric, red chili powder, chaat masala, cumin, coriander, salt, amchur, black pepper) to the bowl with melted ghee
2. Mix well to form a paste
3. Add raw cashews and toss until evenly coated with the spice mixture

### 3. Bake
1. Spread the coated cashews in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet
2. Bake for 12-15 minutes, stirring halfway through
3. Watch carefully to prevent burning - they should be golden brown
4. Remove from oven and let cool completely
5. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired

## Tips
- Watch the cashews carefully during the last few minutes to prevent burning
- Adjust spice levels to your preference
- Let cool completely before storing for best crunch
- Can also be made in an air fryer at 300°F for 8-10 minutes
- Store in an airtight container to maintain freshness

## Notes
- Great for snacking, holiday parties, or work lunches
- Completely vegetarian and dairy-free if using coconut oil
- Can substitute almonds or mixed nuts for variety
